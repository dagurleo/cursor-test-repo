Indoor enrichment

Make the home work like cat territory.

Enrichment is not decoration. It is a set of choices that help a cat feel secure, hunt through play, rest undisturbed, and move through the home without constant competition.

Photo: morefun boy on Unsplash

Start with safe places

Give cats elevated perches, covered beds, or quiet rooms where they can withdraw. A reliable retreat lowers pressure during noise, visitors, conflict, or normal household movement.

Separate key resources

Food, water, litter, scratching areas, play areas, and sleeping places should not be crowded into one corner. Multiple resource locations are especially important in multi-cat homes because they reduce blocking and competition.

Protect play and scent

Daily interactive play gives cats an outlet for predatory behavior. Scent also matters: avoid over-cleaning familiar resting spots all at once, and let cats keep stable scent marks where they rub and scratch.
