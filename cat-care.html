Care basics

Build the daily routine first.

Cats do best when the basics are boring in the right way: predictable meals, clean water, a usable litter setup, safe handling, and routine preventive care.

Image: mission-provided cat-sunlit-window.png

Food and water

Choose a complete cat food matched to the cat's life stage, and ask a veterinarian to adjust portions for age, activity, and health. Fresh water should stay available all day, with bowls washed and refilled daily.

Litter and rest

Keep litter boxes quiet, accessible, and clean. In multi-level homes, place a box on each floor. Give each cat a dry resting spot with washable bedding, and avoid moving essential resources abruptly.

Grooming and health

Regular brushing helps reduce shedding and hairballs. Nail trims every few weeks can reduce damage from normal scratching. Schedule a veterinary exam at least once a year, and call sooner for ongoing vomiting, diarrhea, appetite loss, lethargy, illness, or injury.
