Behavior signals

Respond to changes before they become patterns.

Cat behavior is easier to handle when it is treated as information. Scratching, hiding, toileting changes, and grooming shifts can point to environmental stress, pain, illness, or unmet daily needs.

Image: mission-provided british-shorthair.png

Scratching is normal

Scratching removes old nail sheaths, stretches the body, and leaves visual and scent marks. Offer sturdy vertical and horizontal scratchers near places the cat already scratches.

Litter changes need attention

A cat avoiding the litter box may be reacting to box location, litter type, cleanliness, household conflict, or a medical problem. Sudden changes deserve a veterinary conversation.

Watch grooming and withdrawal

Cats naturally groom often, but hair loss, skin lesions, or a sudden increase in licking can indicate itch, pain, or stress. Hiding more than usual can also be a signal to check health and home conditions.
